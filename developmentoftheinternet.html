History of the Internet

1950s

1958

ARPA (Advanced Research Projects Agency) created by Dwight D. Eisenhower in response to Soviet Union’s Sputnik launch.

 

1959

First Commercial Modem from Bell Labs.

1960s

1961

ASCII (American Standard Code for Information Interchange) begins.

 

1965

MIT – First communication between 2 computers using packet switching technology.

 

1969

First Wide Area Network (WAN) using dialup – ARPANET - created between 3 Universities in California and University of Utah

1970s

1972

First email sent

 

1973

Internet born as University of London and Royal Radar Establishment Norway connect to ARPANET

 

1974

First Commercial internet provider of ARPANET – Telenet founded.

 

1974

TCP (Transmission Control Protocol) designed.

 

1978

I.P. address registry recorded

 

1979

USENET founded to host news and discussion groups

1980s

1981

National Science Foundation funded the Computer Science Network (CSNET) to build network for scientists

 

1982

TCP/IP Internet Protocol adopted by ARPANET

 

1983

DNS (Domain Name System) for registering websites created .com, .gov, .edu used to avoid problems with numbers.

 

1985

First Registered domain – Symbolics.com

 

1987

Number of internet connections passes 20,000

 

1987

Cisco delivers first router as internet expands

 

1989

World.std.com is the first commercial provider of dial up access to the internet.

 

1989

Tim Berners-Lee develops HTTP – Hypertext Transfer Protocol as a standard for hosting and transferring files between server and user

1990s

1990

Tim Berners-Lee develops HTML – a language to create websites – It is still with us today

 

1991

World Wide Web open to the public

 

1992

First audio and video sent over internet

 

1993

600 websites on the internet – 2 million connections to it

 

1993

Mosaic is the first web browser

 

1994

Netscape founded and Windows develops Explorer. Browser war ensues

 

1994

Yahoo is founded

 

1995

Number of Internet Providers grows as Prodigy, AOL and CompuServe are founded

 

1995

Amazon, EBay and Craigslist are launched as internet moves to purely commercial space

 

1995

First dating site match.com launched

 

1995

First Newspaper – The Telegraph available on line

 

1996

Nokia 9000 phone sold as the first phone with Internet Connectivity.

 

1997

Netflix founded as a provider of DVDs through the USPS

 

1997

First Blog by Ian Ring

 

1998

Google search engine launched providing users with a new word and way of interacting with internet.

 

1998

I.P. Version 6 introduced to allow for the registration of trillions of websites

 

1998

Paypal founded giving internet users a new means of paying for purchases

 

1999

Napster launched to facilitate file sharing – MP3 technology had been developed to transfer audio files.

2000s

2000

The .com bubble bursts as many online companies go out of business

 

2000

Who owns what and how content posted on the internet is used becomes a big issue with Napster and other file sharers exploiting the loophole.

 

2002

ISPs offer Wi-Fi connectivity along with cable connectivity – this has rapidly developed over the years from 1st generation to 5th generation to allow people all over connect to the internet as speeds and security levels increase.

 

2003

The blog publishing platform WordPress is launched

 

2004

Facebook begins the social media era

 

2005

YouTube launched

 

2006

Twitter provides a different form of social media.

 

2008

Google updates their search engine with the development of Chrome

 

2008

HTML 5 – the last major update to HTML – is launched. Websites have become more visually appealing and interactivity is encouraged.

2010s

2010

Facebook reaches 400 million users

 

2010

Pinterest and Instagram become the latest social media sites on the internet

 

2012

A bill which would have obligated ISPs to monitor the availability of copyrighted content on unauthorised sites was defeated.

 

2013

It’s not all good – Edward Snowden exposes the National Security Administration’s extensive ability to monitor the communications of practically the whole world. Nothing is private anymore.

 

2014

Retail ecommerce tops 1 Trillion dollars

 

2014

Apple Pay introduced allowing for easier mobile payments through the iphone

 

2015

Instagram reaches 400 million users
Twitter has 316 million users

 

2016

AI voice assistants and the beginnings of the internet of all things – Google assistant, Amazon’s Alexa and Apple’s Siri are introduced.

 

2018

4 billion internet users

 

2018

TikTok becomes available worldwide after being exclusive to Chinese users since 2016

2020s

2020

Retail ecommerce tops 4 trillion dollars

 

2020

Covid 19 Pandemic – accelerates move from bricks and mortar to online activity

 

2020

Movement away from traditional financial institutions – creation of cryptocurrencies and new means of transferring cash such as Revolut and Venmo

 

2020

Blockchain technology developed to revolutionise the way in which the web is structured – movement away from the giants of the internet such as Facebook and Google to a more democratic web.

 

2020

Emphasis on the use of online business meeting tools such as Teams and Zoom – both created in the 2010s but Covid brought them to the fore.

 

2020

Further advances in mobile technology – greater speeds and security with 5th generation.

 

2025

Internet Sales expected to top 7.5 trillion dollars
